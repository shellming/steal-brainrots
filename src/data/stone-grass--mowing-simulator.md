## How to Play Stone Grass: Mowing Simulator

Stone Grass: Mowing Simulator blends the satisfying feedback of a cleanup game with the strategic growth of an incremental tycoon. Whether you are looking to unwind after a long day or build a lawn-care empire, understanding the core loop is essential for maximum efficiency.

### Master the Controls and Movement
Getting started is intuitive, designed to let you focus on the "zen" of the trim. You control a high-powered tractor equipped with rotating blades. To move across the map, simply use the **WASD keys** or **Arrow keys**. If you prefer a more laid-back experience, you can also **click and drag the left mouse button** to guide your mower. The physics are smooth, allowing you to glide through thick overgrowth with ease.

### The Core Gameplay Loop
The objective is simple: clear every blade of grass in sight. As you drive over overgrown patches, your mower automatically harvests the grass and converts it into compact hay bales stored on your trailer. 

1.  **Mow:** Navigate through the wild fields, targeting the densest patches first.
2.  **Collect:** Watch your storage meter. As you cut, your trailer will fill up with resources.
3.  **Sell:** Once your capacity is reached, navigate back to your base station. Your collected grass will be automatically sold for coins.
4.  **Upgrade:** Use your hard-earned profits at the upgrade station to improve your machinery, then head back out to tackle even tougher terrain.

### Expanding Your Empire
You start in a modest field, but the world of Stone Grass is vast. As you clear specific percentages of a map, you will earn enough capital to unlock "Expansion Gates." Passing through these gates reveals new biomes with thicker grass and higher sell values. To keep up with these new challenges, you must constantly balance your budget between **Speed** (to move faster), **Power** (to cut through dense brush), and **Capacity** (to stay in the field longer). Success is measured by how quickly you can turn a chaotic wilderness into a manicured masterpiece.

## Tips & Strategies for Stone Grass: Mowing Simulator

To build a thriving landscaping empire, you need more than just a sharp blade; you need a solid plan. Follow these expert strategies to maximize your efficiency and turn those wild meadows into cold, hard cash.

### Prioritize Early Upgrades
In the early game, your biggest bottlenecks are movement speed and storage capacity. Focus your initial earnings on boosting your **Truck Capacity** and **Engine Speed**. Increasing your capacity allows you to stay in the field longer, while higher speed ensures you aren't wasting time crawling back to the silo. Once these are stable, invest in your **Saws** to cut through denser patches with ease.

### Optimize Your Mowing Path
Don't just mow randomly. To maximize your efficiency, try to mow in long, continuous lines or concentric circles. This minimizes the time spent turning and ensures you aren't leaving small, annoying tufts of grass behind. Remember, any time spent driving over already-cut dirt is time you aren't earning money.

### Maximize Every Trip
It can be tempting to head back to the base as soon as you see your progress bar filling up, but efficiency is key. Always wait until your storage is at **100% capacity** before returning to the sell point. This reduces the total number of trips you take over the course of a level, allowing you to spend more time doing what matters: clearing the field.

### Focus on Expansion Zones
As soon as you have enough funds to unlock a new zone, do it. New areas often contain denser grass or larger fields that yield higher returns. Expanding your territory is the fastest way to scale your earnings and unlock the more advanced mower upgrades required for late-game challenges.

### Keep the Momentum
Stone Grass is an incremental game at its core, meaning idle time is your biggest enemy. Stay in constant motion. If you find your tractor slowing down significantly, it’s a sign that the grass in that area is too thick for your current blade level. Instead of struggling through it, pivot to lighter patches to finish filling your tank, then use those profits to upgrade your power.

### Watch for Special Patches
Keep an eye out for variations in the terrain. Some patches of overgrowth are denser than others and provide a higher volume of resources per square inch. Targeting these "high-yield" spots early in your run can help you hit your storage limit faster, leading to quicker upgrade cycles.

## FAQ about Stone Grass: Mowing Simulator

### How do I earn money quickly in Stone Grass?
To maximize your earnings, focus on clearing dense patches of grass and returning to your base once your storage is completely full. Investing early in speed and capacity upgrades will allow you to process more grass per minute, significantly increasing your cash flow as you progress through different zones.

### What are the best upgrades to prioritize first?
The most efficient strategy is to prioritize your **saws** and **truck capacity**. Upgrading your saws allows you to cut through thick overgrowth without slowing down, while increasing your capacity reduces the frequency of trips back to the base, keeping you in the "flow" of mowing for longer periods.

### How do I unlock new islands and territories?
New areas are unlocked by reaching specific financial milestones and completing the current field. Once you have mowed enough grass and sold it at the base, you will earn the funds required to clear obstacles or pay for the bridge to the next zone, where the grass is tougher but more valuable.

### Why is my mower slowing down while cutting?
If your tractor feels sluggish, it is usually due to one of two reasons: your storage is reaching its limit, or the grass in the current area is too dense for your current saw level. Head back to the upgrade station to boost your power and speed to regain that smooth, satisfying movement.

### Are there different types of grass to collect?
Yes! As you expand your territory, you will encounter various types of vegetation. Some patches are much denser and require more power to cut through, but these provide a higher volume of resources, making them the most profitable areas to target once your equipment is sufficiently upgraded.
